# NSW Local Government Procurement Compliance System

## Overview
This AI-Powered Procurement Automation Platform includes comprehensive compliance features to ensure full adherence to NSW Local Government procurement guidelines, WHS, ADR, and all relevant standards.

## Compliance Features

### 1. Comprehensive RAG System
- **Retrieval-Augmented Generation** for compliance checking
- **Real-time compliance validation** against NSW Local Government requirements
- **Automated compliance scoring** and risk assessment
- **Evidence-based compliance reporting**

### 2. NSW Local Government Act 1993 Compliance
- **Section 55**: Tendering requirements for contracts over $150,000
- **Section 55A**: Tender evaluation criteria compliance
- **Section 55B**: Exemptions from tendering requirements
- **Probity and transparency** requirements

### 3. Work Health and Safety (WHS) Compliance
- **AS/NZS 4801:2001**: Occupational Health and Safety Management Systems
- **AS/NZS 45001:2018**: ISO 45001 certification requirements
- **WHS Management Plans** for construction projects
- **Risk assessment** and safety management

### 4. Australian Design Rules (ADR) Compliance
- **ADR 1/00**: Vehicle Standards
- **ADR 2/00**: Side Door Strength
- **ADR 3/00**: Seat Anchorages
- **ADR 4/00**: Seat Belt Anchorages
- **ADR 5/00**: Anchorages for Child Restraints

### 5. Environmental Management Compliance
- **AS/NZS ISO 14001:2016**: Environmental Management Systems
- **Environmental Impact Assessment** requirements
- **Waste Management Plans** for construction
- **Sustainability considerations** in procurement

### 6. Quality Management Compliance
- **AS/NZS ISO 9001:2016**: Quality Management Systems
- **Quality assurance** processes
- **Performance standards** and criteria
- **Continuous improvement** requirements

## Compliance Checklist

### Pre-Tender
- [ ] Needs analysis completed
- [ ] Budget approved
- [ ] Risk assessment conducted
- [ ] Evaluation criteria defined
- [ ] Probity advisor appointed (if required)
- [ ] Tender documentation prepared

### Tender Process
- [ ] Public notification published
- [ ] Tender period adequate
- [ ] Information sessions conducted (if required)
- [ ] Questions and answers documented
- [ ] Addenda issued (if required)
- [ ] Tender submissions received and secured

### Evaluation
- [ ] Evaluation panel appointed
- [ ] Evaluation criteria applied consistently
- [ ] Scoring methodology followed
- [ ] Documentation maintained
- [ ] Probity requirements met
- [ ] Evaluation report prepared

### Award
- [ ] Successful tenderer selected
- [ ] Contract negotiations completed
- [ ] Contract executed
- [ ] Unsuccessful tenderers notified
- [ ] Debriefing offered
- [ ] Records archived

## Risk Management

### Risk Categories
1. **Financial Risk**: Supplier stability, payment terms, insurance
2. **Operational Risk**: Delivery timelines, quality standards, capacity
3. **Compliance Risk**: Regulatory compliance, WHS, environmental
4. **Reputational Risk**: Public perception, media attention, community impact

### Risk Assessment Process
1. **Identify risks** associated with procurement
2. **Assess likelihood** and impact of each risk
3. **Develop mitigation strategies** for identified risks
4. **Monitor and review** risk management effectiveness
