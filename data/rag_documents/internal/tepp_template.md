## SECTION 3: PROBITY AND ACCOUNTABILITY

### 3.1 PROBITY

**Core Principles:**
1. ensure conformity to processes that are designed to achieve best value for money;
2. improve accountability;
3. encourage commercial competition on the basis that all Tenders will be assessed against the same criteria;
4. preserve public and Tenderer confidence in government processes; and
5. improve defensibility of decisions so as to avoid potential administrative and legal challenge.

**Five Essential Principles:**
1. open competitive process,
2. transparency of process,
3. identification and resolution of conflicts of interest,
4. accountability, and
5. monitoring and evaluating performance.

### 3.2 CONFIDENTIALITY AND CONFLICT OF INTEREST

**Confidential Information:**
- Contents of submissions from Tenderers
- Clarification questions and responses
- Confidential information produced as part of the evaluation process (e.g. scoresheets, meeting minutes and evaluation reports)
- Other information related to the process that is not publicly available.

**Conflict of Interest Requirements:**
- Declaration of relationships or connections with Tenderers
- No acceptance of gifts, meals, etc. from interested parties
- Immediate declaration of any real or perceived conflict of interest
- Replacement of committee members if conflict cannot be resolved

### 3.3 SECURITY AND CONFIDENTIALITY

**Security Requirements:**
- Confidential information should not be removed from Council's office
- Safe keeping responsibility when removal is necessary
- Password-controlled computer directories
- Secure environment requirements

### 3.4 AUTHORISED CONTACT OFFICER

**Template:**
As stated in the Tender documentation, the only Council officer who is authorised to deal with enquiries is [INSERT NAME AND TITLE OF PERSON] that will field questions on the tender in your team.

**Contact Officer Responsibilities:**
- Document all contact with prospective Tenderers
- Provide consistent information uniformly to all Tenderers
- Limit information to clarification of procedural issues
- Ensure any additional information provided to one is provided to all
- No disclosure of information giving unfair advantage

### 3.5 ADVERTISING THE RFT

**Standard Advertising:**
- Council's website 'Tenders Section'
- WSROC NSW e-tendering website
- Sydney Morning Herald (Tuesday and Saturday)
- Local newspaper(s) for two weeks (Tuesday and Wednesday)

### 3.6 RECEIPT OF TENDERS

**Electronic Lodgement:**
- Tenders must be lodged electronically at the Tendering website
- Must be submitted before the closing date and time
- Opened concurrently by representatives from Internal Audit and Governance

### 3.7 LATE TENDERS

**Late Tender Policy:**
Late tenders will not be accepted unless there is sufficient evidence that:
- was delivered to the specified location before the specified closing time; or
- was dispatched in sufficient time before the specified closing time for tenders, to reach that place under normal circumstances, but was still in the course of delivery at the specified closing time.

### 3.8 REQUESTS FOR CLARIFICATION

**Clarification Process:**
- Written clarification requests and responses
- Early identification of aspects requiring clarification
- Coordination by Authorised Contact Officer
- No revision of original offers permitted
- No new or additional information requested

### 3.9 CRITICAL ISSUES OR RISKS

**Risk Management Table:**
| Issue/Risk | Consequences | Action |
|------------|--------------|--------|
| Pre-selection of preferred tenderer | Does not provide value to Council and is a risk of potential corruption | Independent evaluation without influence from personnel who may have existing relationship with contractors |
| Poor quality at high cost | Inflated costs for sub-standard service | Evaluation includes subject matter of expertise |

## SECTION 7: PRICING

### 7.1.1 PRICING MODEL

**Price Normalization Formula:**
```
Pc = Total Net Cost calculated for tender evaluation
Pav = Average of all Total Net Costs
Ps = Price score = 200 - (1001 x Pc/Pav)
Pn = Normalised Price Score = Ps/Highest Ps x 1001
Pw = Weighted Price Score = Pn x percentage weighting/100
```

### 7.1.2 LOCAL PREFERENCE POLICY

**Blacktown City Local Supplier:**
- 5% discount on tendered pricing
- Capped at maximum benefit of $50,000
- Principal place of business within Blacktown City LGA

**Western Sydney Council Supplier:**
- 2.5% discount on tendered pricing
- Capped at maximum benefit of $25,000
- Principal place of business within Western Sydney Council LGA

**Eligible Western Sydney Councils:**
- Blue Mountains City Council
- City of Parramatta Council
- Cumberland City Council
- Fairfield City Council
- Hawkesbury City Council
- Liverpool City Council
- Parramatta City Council
- Penrith City Council
- The Hills Shire Council

### 7.1.3 VALUE FOR MONEY

**Assessment Factors:**
- Quality of proposed provision of services
- How well it meets specified requirements
- Whole of life costs
- Risk assessment
- Capacity to deliver services on-time and on-budget

### 7.1.4 INTERVIEWS AND PRESENTATION CRITERIA

**Process:**
- Final scores may be revised following interviews/presentations
- Conducted with shortlisted Tenders
- In accordance with section 6.4 Tenderer Presentations

### 7.2 SHORT-LISTING / SETTING ASIDE TENDERS

**Exclusion Criteria:**
- Significant number of Tenders received
- Clearly non-competitive Tenders
- No reasonable prospect of exhibiting best value for money
- Reasons for exclusion must be defensible

### 7.3 ALTERNATIVE TENDERS

**Evaluation:**
- Alternative Tenders evaluated in accordance with evaluation methodology
- Specified in the RFT and reproduced in this document

### 7.4 TENDERER PRESENTATIONS

**Process:**
- Tenderers may be invited to attend interviews
- Opportunity for Evaluation Committee to ask questions
- No new or additional information requested or permitted
- Factual answers in unbiased manner required

### 7.5 EVALUATION REPORT

**Report Contents:**
- Summary of evaluation method
- Number of Tenders received
- Relative ranking of Tenders
- Recommendation as to preferred Tender
- Rationale for selection of preferred supplier
- Key issues requiring negotiation/ongoing scrutiny

## SECTION 8: CONTRACT NEGOTIATIONS

**Negotiation Process:**
- Period of negotiation with successful Tenderer
- Commences subject to approval by Director
- Formal written obligations to be resolved
- Appropriate to nature of project
- Open and fair approach
- Meets needs of Tenderer
- Accommodated within Tenderer's resources

**Negotiation Limitations:**
- Must not result in material changes to Tender requirements
- Outcome reflected in final contract
- All discussions and outcomes documented

## SECTION 9: DEBRIEFING OF UNSUCCESSFUL TENDERERS

**Debriefing Process:**
- Arranged for any unsuccessful Tenderers who request it
- General comments regarding evaluation against criteria
- Conducted by at least two members of Evaluation Committee
- May be carried out by telephone or letter
- Limited to unsuccessful Tenderer's offer
- No comparisons with successful Tender
- No scoring details provided

## AI Customization Instructions

When generating a TEPP for a specific project, the AI should:

1. **Extract Project Information**:
   - Project title and description
   - Contract value and duration
   - Project type and category
   - Council/organization details
   - Technical requirements

2. **Determine Process Type**:
   - Under $100k: Quotation process
   - $100k-$250k: Tender process
   - $250k+: Formal Tender process

3. **Customize Content**:
   - Replace all placeholder text with project-specific information
   - Adjust evaluation criteria weights based on project type
   - Set appropriate timelines and deadlines
   - Include project-specific technical requirements
   - Add relevant risk factors and mitigation strategies

4. **Ensure Compliance**:
   - Follow Local Government Act requirements
   - Include all mandatory sections
   - Maintain probity and accountability standards
   - Ensure evaluation methodology is appropriate

5. **Make Editable**:
   - All generated content should be easily editable
   - Provide clear guidance for modifications
   - Maintain document structure and formatting
   - Enable approval workflow integration
